Research question

How can a pretrained VLA become a reliable policy for a previously unseen robot embodiment?

FlowBridge investigates parameter-efficient adaptation from limited local demonstrations with a modular safety layer around learned action generation.

Contributions

Three connected contributions define Stage 1.

01 · Efficient adaptation

π0.5 initialization with localized late-stage SigLIP LoRA and action-expert adaptation.

02 · Explicit robot contract

A verified 16 × 7 interface of six joint-position deltas and one absolute suction command.

03 · Physical execution evidence

ROS2-supervised physical evaluation on a Dobot Magician E6.

Research evolution

From an initial question to an extended journal study.

Phase 01

Problem definition

Adapt a pretrained VLA to a heterogeneous 6-DoF robot arm using limited local demonstrations.

Phase 02

Dobot E6 dataset

Synchronized HIK and ZED observations, robot state, suction state, actions, and prompts were assembled into a local training dataset.

Phase 03

Pi-Zero conference study

Local fine-tuning, ROS-based control, object and color conditioning, pick-and-place evaluation, and ServoJ/MovJ comparison formed the KSCI conference milestone.

Phase 04

π0.5 / v23 system extension

The representative implementation moved to π0.5 initialization with SigLIP blocks 18–26 LoRA, action-expert adaptation, a 16-step horizon, and a 7D external action contract.

Phase 05

JKSCI journal extension

The extended manuscript is in preparation. Its title and status are public, while the draft PDF remains private until submission.
