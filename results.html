Two-stage interpretation

Target selection succeeded before two manipulation failures.

The 20 pick-and-place trials are evaluated at both the target-approach stage and the final task-completion stage.

Prompt-conditioned target approach20 / 20Correct target-directed trajectory
Complete pick-and-place18 / 20Grasp, lift, transport, descent, and placement
Post-approach manipulation failure2 / 20Failure occurred after correct target approach

Across all 20 pick-and-place trials, the π0.5 policy generated a target-directed approach trajectory toward the object specified by the prompt. Eighteen trials subsequently completed suction grasping, deterministic post-grasp lifting, prompt-conditioned transport and descent, and final placement. The remaining two failures therefore belong to the physical manipulation stage rather than initial target selection or approach.

Evaluation set

Four colors and four object categories.

Each color and each object category was evaluated in 20 trials, producing 80 trials per conditioned-approach evaluation.
